Introduction¶

Dataset Description¶

In this project, we will be investigating the No-show Appointments dataset. It contains a 100k recored of medical appointments in Brazil and is mainly focused around if the patient made it to the appointment or not. We will be looking into patterns of the no shows and other related attributes.

  1. PatientId: Identification number for each patient.

  2. AppointmentID: Identification number for each appointment made.

  3. Gender: Is the patient Male or Female.

  4. ScheduledDay: The day someone registered/called to make the appointment.

  5. AppointmentDay: The actual day the patient has to visit the doctor.

  6. Age: How old the patient is.

  7. Neighbourhood: The location of the hospital/clinic.

  8. Scholarship:whether or not the patient is enrolled in a Brasilian welfare program that provides financial aid. 0 or 1 for no or yes. [ref:](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bolsa_Fam%C3%ADlia)

  9. Hipertension: 0 or 1 for no or yes.

  10. Diabetes: 0 or 1 for no or yes.

  11. Handcap: 0 or 1 for no or yes.

  12. SMS_received: If messages were sent to the patient for a reminder. 0 or 1 for no or yes.

  13. No-show: Whether the patient made it to the appointment or not. Yes for no-show, No for showing up.
    14. </ol ref: [https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/joniarroba/noshowappointments/code]

Question(s) for Analysis¶

  1. What is the age range of the patients who did not come to their appointments?
  2. How is gender associated with showing up for an appointment?
  3. Does being handcap affect attendance of appointments?
  4. How many people received an SMS and did not show up to the appointments?
  5. How many people had scholarships and failed to show up for their appointments?

Conclusions¶

Out of the total 110,522 appointment records provided in the dataset, and after some cleaning, 22273 did not show up for their appointments

More women register for appointments, but the percentage of no shows in both genders are almost the same

Increasing the number of SMS's sent may increase the number of patients who can attend their appointments.

Older people are more likely to show up for their appointments than younger people. Having a scholarship doesn't really affect whether a patient will show up for their appointment or not. The age of the patients also doesn't have a significant effect on the attendance. The analysis, however, does not imply that one change causes another.
